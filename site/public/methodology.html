SOXX Regime Monitor — Methodology

This page documents the signal construction, state machine rules, and backtest assumptions underlying the dashboard.

0. The System in Ten Lines

  1. The core signal is the overnight/intraday return decomposition: who is actually buying — institutions (intraday, open→close) or Asia/futures gaps (overnight, close→open)?
  2. When price rises but the 20-day intraday stream (id20) is negative, institutions are selling into the overnight bid. That is distribution — the system arms (→ MONITOR state).
  3. When price falls but id20 is strongly positive, institutions are buying the decline. That is accumulation (ACCUM overlay). Stay long or re-enter.
  4. Once in MONITOR, de-risk only into strength — on the next +1% intraday or +1.5% day (→ EXIT state). Never into a panic close. This single execution rule was worth ≈20 return points in the Jun 2026 exit.
  5. Realized vol does not trigger anything. It sizes the position (vol-target / RV envelope). Full exposure below rv20_p90; scaled back only in extreme regimes.
  6. Implied vol does not trigger anything. It selects the instrument: IV rich vs. RV → sell shares; IV cheap vs. RV → hold shares, buy puts instead.
  7. Fundamentals (TSMC revenue, memory pricing, hyperscaler capex) do not trigger anything. They inform the maximum strategic-capital cap, reviewed monthly (see §9).
  8. Moving averages are confirmation and re-entry lines only (20-DMA), never the primary signal.
  9. In 2026 this triggered three times: a small Feb whipsaw, then a clean EXIT at $639 on Jun 18 — four days before the $655 all-time high — flat through the entire June–July slide.
  10. See §11 for current state and re-entry conditions. See §10 for the full 2026 performance record.

1. Overview

The monitor tracks a return-decomposition signal on SOXX (iShares Semiconductor ETF). Each daily return is split into:

  • Overnight (on) = log(opent / closet-1) — Asia-session institutional flow and overnight futures (see K-findings below)
  • Intraday (id) = log(closet / opent) — US-session institutional intraday flow

A 20-session rolling sum of each gives on20 and id20. Persistent negative id20 while on20 stays positive signals distribution: US-session institutions selling into the overnight bid. This divergence precedes drawdowns.

K-findings (verified 2026 YTD): The overnight channel is Asia-institutional, not retail. Regressing SOXX overnight returns on TSM, EWY, and QQQ overnight returns explains R²=0.89 of variance; pairwise: corr(SOXX_on, TSM_on)=+0.85, corr(SOXX_on, EWY_on)=+0.82. TSM's 2026 YTD decomposition: +37pts overnight (Taiwan-session price discovery lands in the US gap window) vs. +6pts intraday. The structural positive bias in on20 is therefore Asia-session drift — which is why on20 is interpreted relative to itself rather than zero-anchored. The Jun 2026 divergence is correctly read as US desks distributing into an Asian institutional bid; the Korea-led overnight fade (Jun 23–Jul 1: SOXX on-sum −7.4%, EWY −13.4%) is more informative than any "retail tiring" framing.

2. Defined Terms

All metrics used in this dashboard, in plain English.

Daily Return Components

on overnight return
The price change from the previous day's close to today's open — what happened while the US market was shut.
Formula: log(opent / closet−1). Predominantly driven by Asia-session institutional order flow (Taiwan and Korean semiconductor markets trade while the US is closed) and overnight futures positioning. Not retail — see K-findings in §1.
id intraday return
The price change from today's open to today's close — what happened during the US trading session.
Formula: log(closet / opent). Dominated by US-session institutional order flow. This is the primary signal: net positive id20 = US institutions buying intraday; net negative = US institutions distributing.
ret total daily return
The full day's log-return: previous close to current close. Equal to on + id.

Rolling Stream Signals

id20 20-day intraday stream
The sum of the last 20 intraday returns. This is the core decision signal.
When negative, institutions have been net sellers during the trading day over the past month, even if the stock is up. This is the distribution signal that arms the monitor.
Known confound — dealer delta hedging: in negative-gamma regimes, market-maker delta hedging mechanically sells weakness during regular hours, depressing idt independent of discretionary institutional flow. id20 therefore measures net US-regular-hours flow from all participants and cannot distinguish discretionary distribution from dealer-hedging amplification. This confound is currently untestable with free data (no public SOXX GEX history; the only GEX-calibrated strategy tested in an independent 2026 review underperformed buy-and-hold by 4pts in its own table). The options_history.csv bootstrap (v3.2 Change 6) is the designated test path if GEX data becomes available.
on20 20-day overnight stream
The sum of the last 20 overnight returns. Captures the Asia-institutional overnight bid (structural drift from Taiwan and Korean markets).
When on20 stays positive while id20 turns negative, US-session institutions are distributing into the Asia-institutional bid — the defining divergence pattern. When the Asia bid itself fades (tracked via on20_mom), the divergence is resolving.
ret20 20-day total return
The cumulative log-return over the last 20 sessions. Measures the current 1-month trend.
Used in the arm condition (distribution is only flagged when the trend is still up) and the accumulation overlay (price falling with id20 positive).

Volatility Metrics

rv20 20-day realized volatility
The annualized standard deviation of daily log-returns over the past 20 sessions.
Used to scale position size: full exposure only when volatility is below ~50% annualized. High volatility = smaller suggested size.
rv10 10-day realized volatility
Same as rv20 but over 10 sessions — a shorter-term volatility gauge, more sensitive to recent spikes.
iv30 30-day implied volatility
The market's expectation of volatility over the next 30 days, derived from options prices. Entered manually from options market data.
vrp variance risk premium
Implied vol minus realized vol: iv30 − rv20.
Positive = options are expensive relative to recent moves (normal; sellers get paid a premium). Negative = options are cheap, which often happens during sharp sell-offs when realized vol spikes above implied. Negative VRP suggests hedging with puts is cheap.

v3.3 Asia Diagnostics

gap_quality overnight gap classification
Classifies each SOXX session as: none (no overnight gap > +0.3%), confirmed (SOXX gapped AND TSM or EWY also gapped > +0.3% — Asia-backed), or hollow (SOXX gapped but neither TSM nor EWY gapped — US-futures only).
Of 65 SOXX up-gaps in 2026 YTD, 62 were Asia-confirmed and 3 were hollow. The 3 hollow gaps (2026-03-30, 04-20, 04-23) faded −1.29% same-day intraday on average vs. +0.46% follow-through for confirmed gaps. Diagnostic only (n=3 on hollow side).
asia_on20 Asia overnight 20d stream
The equal-weighted 20-session sum of TSM and EWY overnight returns: 0.5 × tsm_on + 0.5 × ewy_on, rolling 20.
Shown as an optional overlay on the decomposition chart to separate the Asia institutional component of SOXX's overnight return. Context only — not a state machine input.
hollow_count20 hollow gap count (trailing 20d)
Number of hollow up-gaps in the past 20 sessions. A rising count while id20 is negative adds qualitative weight to the distribution read — Asia is no longer endorsing the US gap-ups.

v3.2 Derived Series

id20_z id20 z-score
How many standard deviations id20 is below its own 252-session rolling mean. Requires a minimum of 120 sessions of history.
Formula: (id20 − rolling_mean(id20, 252)) / rolling_std(id20, 252). A value of −1.0 or below is the Mode B arm threshold (see §4.1).
on20_mom overnight momentum
The 5-session change in the overnight stream: on20 minus on20 five sessions ago.
When this turns negative in MONITOR state (threshold: −5%), it means the overnight bid is fading — institutions are no longer supporting the gap-up. Used as a sizing tilt only; never as a transition trigger (see §5).
dd20 20-session drawdown
How far the current close is below the highest close over the past 20 sessions: close / max(close, 20) − 1. Always ≤ 0.
Used by the optional CRASH_GATE flag to identify when the market is already in a deep decline (≤ −10%), suppressing the ABS arm to avoid a panic-exit at the low.
vix_slope VIX term structure
VIX 3-month (VXVCLS) minus VIX spot (VIXCLS), sourced from FRED. Positive = VIX3M > VIX = term structure in contango (normal, calm conditions). Negative = inversion = near-term fear exceeds longer-term fear (stress signal).
Coincident indicator only — not a direct input to state transitions. A positive slope during a sell-off suggests the correction is orderly rather than panicked.
real_chg20 real yield change
The 20-session change in the US 10-year real yield (nominal yield DGS10 minus 10-year breakeven inflation T10YIE), both from FRED. Expressed in percentage points; displayed in basis points (1pp = 100bp).
Context indicator for growth/tech sector headwinds. Rising real yields compress valuations of long-duration assets like semiconductors. Not used in sizing or transitions.

Trend & Momentum Indicators

ma20 / ma50 / ma200 moving averages
Simple moving averages of closing prices over 20, 50, and 200 sessions respectively.
ma20 is the re-entry line: price must recover above it (for 2 consecutive sessions with positive id20) to trigger a trend-reclaim re-entry from EXIT state.
rsi14 relative strength index
A momentum oscillator (0–100) measuring the speed and magnitude of recent price changes over 14 sessions.
Above 70 = overbought; below 30 = oversold. Used as a supplementary context indicator — not a direct input to the state machine.
dist20 sessions since MA20 cross
How many trading sessions have elapsed since price last crossed above or below the 20-day moving average.
A large number means price has been persistently above or below the line — indicating a more entrenched trend.
ar1 return autocorrelation
The first-order autocorrelation of daily returns over the last 20 sessions.
Positive = returns are trending (today up → tomorrow likely up). Negative = mean-reverting (today up → tomorrow likely down). Extreme negative values can signal choppy, whipsaw conditions.

Sentiment & Risk Indicators

turb turbulence score
A statistical measure of how unusual today's return is relative to the recent 20-day distribution, expressed as a Z-score.
A score above 3 means today's move is more than 3 standard deviations from recent average — a tail event. The Jun 5, 2026 crash (−10.4%) scored ~3.7.
pc_oi put/call open interest ratio
The total open interest of put options divided by call options on SOXX (entered manually from options market data).
High ratio = more puts outstanding than calls = bearish hedging in the market. Useful as a sentiment cross-check; not a direct state machine input.
iv30_asof
The date the IV30 reading was last manually updated. Shown to flag when the options data may be stale.

4. State Machine

The system operates in five states:

StateColourMeaning
WARMUP■ GrayFirst 20 sessions of year — insufficient history
RISK_ON■ GreenFully invested; no distribution signal
MONITOR■ AmberDistribution detected; monitoring for exit trigger
EXIT■ RedFlat / defensive; awaiting re-entry conditions
ACCUM■ BlueOverlay: price weak but intraday flow positive — stay invested

4.1 Arm Condition (RISK_ON → MONITOR)

The arm condition uses a hybrid rule: Mode B OR (Mode A AND collapse gate). Both modes must be evaluated every session; arm fires if either branch is satisfied.

Mode A — Absolute threshold

Mode A has two sub-conditions (either is sufficient):

  • DIV arm: id20 < 0 AND ret20 > +2% — intraday selling has turned net negative while the 20-day trend is still positive. The classic divergence pattern.
  • ABS arm: id20 < −3% — extreme intraday selling regardless of trend direction.

Mode A alone fires too frequently in bull markets (26% of sessions in a benign 2025 grind) because intraday returns are structurally below overnight returns. To filter noise, Mode A requires the collapse gate:

Collapse gate: id20_max60 − id20 ≥ 8% — the current id20 must have fallen at least 8 percentage points from its 60-session high. This ensures Mode A only arms when there has been a genuine deterioration in intraday flow, not a brief dip in an ongoing uptrend.

Mode B — Relative (z-score) threshold

Mode B arm: id20_z < −1.0 AND ret20 > +2% — intraday selling is more than 1 standard deviation below its own 252-session historical mean, while the overall trend is still positive. Mode B is regime-aware: it adjusts its threshold to the current distribution, making it robust in both low- and high-id20 environments. It does not require the collapse gate.

Mode B alone can miss exits where a melt-up inflates the z-score baseline (the Jun 2026 exit had z = −0.62, above the −1.0 threshold). This is why the hybrid combines both modes.

Mode B — Statistical caveat (v3.5)

Z1 — The 252-day baseline spans a verified structural break. id20 in H2-2025 vs. 2026 YTD: mean +1.1% vs. +5.8%, σ 3.9% vs. 6.6%, max +8.3% vs. +23.5%. A two-sample KS test (n=126/113) yields D = 0.37, far above the 1% critical threshold of ≈0.17. These are statistically distinct distributions. A z = −1.6 in July 2026 and a z = −1.6 in February 2026 do not mean the same thing. Do not interpret the magnitude of id20_z as comparable across regimes.

Z2 — But the contamination largely self-cancels in practice. Because the AI boom inflated the baseline mean and standard deviation together, the id20 level implied by z = −1 stayed narrow all year: −3.2% (Feb 4), −2.8% (Apr 24), −2.6% (Jun 9/18), −2.9% (Jul 1). Mode B currently behaves as a soft absolute threshold near −2.7%. Its June non-arms reflect id20 = −2.2% and −0.4% not reaching that line — not baseline drift. The z-score trigger input remains valid; only its interpretation as "unusually extreme vs. history" is regime-contaminated.

Z3 — Tested alternatives are all rejected; the dilemma is structural. With one structural break and 13 months of data, no regime-relative statistic can be both fast (catches the Jun 18 exit) and quiet (no false-positive arms in the H2-2025 grind). Do not revisit these before 2019–2024 SOXX daily data is available for out-of-sample validation:

Hybrid rule summary

armed = Mode B OR (Mode A AND collapse_gate)

When the two modes disagree, the dashboard shows a banner explaining which is active. Arm condition does not fire while ACCUM overlay is active.

4.2 Exit Trigger (MONITOR → EXIT)

Exec-into-strength: idt > +1% OR rett > +1.5% while armed condition is still true.

Same-day arm+exit: if arm and exit conditions both trigger on the same session, exit fires immediately (MONITOR band shown for that day only).

Escape valve: after 3 sessions in MONITOR with no strength day AND close < MA20, exit anyway.

4.3 Disarm (MONITOR → RISK_ON, no trade)

If the arm condition clears (reverses) before an exit executes → cancel, no trade. This takes priority over exec-into-strength on the day the condition clears.

4.4 Accumulation Override (RISK_ON + ACCUM overlay)

id20 > +2% AND ret20 < −2% — price falling but institutions buying intraday. Remain invested; arm condition does not fire during ACCUM.

4.5 Re-entry (EXIT → RISK_ON)

Any of (evaluated in order each close; first to trigger wins):

  • Disarm (canonical, 1-session): arm condition evaluates false at this close → re-enter immediately at this close. Reason: "disarm". Patience on re-entries is expensive — V-bounces front-load their gains (confirmed by erratum v3.7.1 backtest: disarm-1 ≈ +72.9% vs. disarm-2 ≈ +59.7%, Jan 20–Jul 10 2026). The twitchiness concern is mitigated by position sizing (vol-target scaling), not by re-entry delay.
  • Accumulation flip: acc condition (id20 > +2% AND ret20 < −2%) triggers while in EXIT → re-entry. Reason: "accumulation flip".
  • MA20 recovery: 2 consecutive sessions above MA20 while id20 > 0. Reason: "trend reclaim".

4.6 Short Permission

A short position is permitted only when all three conditions are simultaneously true:

  1. Price below 50-DMA on elevated volume: close < MA50 AND volume > 1.5 × vol30 — price has broken the intermediate trend line with institutional participation.
  2. Intraday flow negative: id20 < 0 — institutions are net sellers intraday.
  3. Overnight bid absent: on20 < 0 — retail/news-driven overnight demand has also turned negative, removing the structural overnight support.

All three together indicate a broad-based decline rather than a sector rotation. The dashboard shows a per-condition pass/fail breakdown in the Current State card.

5. Position Sizing

Base size: size_base = min(1, 0.40 / rv20) only when rv20 > rv20_p90 (90th percentile of trailing 252-session rv20); otherwise size_base = 1.0. This scales down only in extreme-volatility regimes, preserving full exposure during normal and low-volatility melt-ups.

on20_mom tilt: When the state is MONITOR and on20_mom < −5% (overnight momentum fading), suggested size is multiplied by 0.8 — a 20% reduction. This is a display-only suggestion; it does not affect state transitions.

State multipliers: RISK_ON = 1.0 (before vol scaling), MONITOR = 0.5, ACCUM = 1.0, EXIT = 0. Final size = size_base × state_multiplier × on20_mom_factor.

6. Backtest Assumptions

  • YTD start = first trading session of Jan 2026; WARMUP = first 20 sessions
  • State machine resets each Jan 1
  • Equity curve: 1× strategy (exit → cash earning 0%), vs 1× B&H
  • Slippage: 5 bps per side applied on each trade date (net equity)
  • Historical window: 600 calendar days for rolling computations (252-session z-score baseline)
  • Fills at same-session close; position earns from next session

8. Known Failure Modes (Verified)

F1 — No gap protection

On 2026-06-04 the system was RISK_ON with id20 = +10.9% (near its most bullish reading). The −10.44% June 5 crash arrived with zero warning. The signal detects distribution processes, not exogenous shocks. Nothing in the methodology fixes this; it is unfixable within this signal framework. Do not claim or expect crash or gap protection from this system.

F2 — Zero-anchor fragility (Mode A)

The absolute arm condition (id20 < 0 AND ret20 > +2%) fired on 33 of 128 sessions (26%) in H2-2025, a benign +27% grind. It over-arms in normal regimes because equity intraday returns are structurally below overnight returns (the overnight-drift anomaly). The hybrid rule adds a 60-session collapse gate to Mode A, requiring id20_max60 − id20 ≥ 8% before Mode A fires.

F3 — Pure z-score arm misses 2026 exit

Anchoring entirely to a 252-session z-score (Mode B) is robust across regimes but does not arm on the Jun 9 or Jun 18, 2026 episodes (z = −0.92 and −0.62; the melt-up inflated the baseline). Mode B alone would exit around $625 vs the actual $639.45. Neither mode alone is acceptable; the hybrid rule combines both.

F4 — ACCUM is unbounded without stops

Without invalidation stops, the ACCUM overlay held full exposure through the −15.8% Feb–Mar drawdown. Correct outcome that episode; unacceptable process in a sustained bear market. Stops added: 8% price drawdown from ACCUM activation close, or id20 rolling back below +1%.

F5 — Execution semantics

Same-day arm+exit is explicitly supported and occurred on Jun 18, 2026 (armed and exited at $639.45 in one session). Fills are assumed at same-session close (MOC order). In practice: never use market orders in the opening auction; SOXX spreads run 2–5 bps intraday, widest at the bells.

Additional notes

  • The escape valve (exit after N sessions armed with no strength day) has never fired in live or backtest data — treat its first activation with suspicion.
  • All thresholds are in-sample choices calibrated on n=3 confirming episodes (Jan–Jul 2026). The hybrid rule is itself a third in-sample iteration. Multi-year (2019–2025) calibration is pending.
  • VRP note: vrp = iv30 − rv20 is a vol-points (practitioner) convention, not variance units. Used only as a hedge-instrument selector, never as a harvesting signal. When hedging with puts: roll at 21–30 DTE, cap annual hedge budget (≤2% of NAV per quarter), and treat 30-day IV as potentially decoupled from short-dated tail pricing in the 0DTE era.

Appendix A — v3.2 Pressure Test Results

The following pressure tests (P1–P6) were run against the Jan–Jul 2026 fixture (n=128 sessions, 3 confirming episodes) as part of the v3.2 change order. Tests V1–V9 are pinned in pipeline/tests/test_state_machine.py.

P1 — Order-of-Operations Pin

Verified that on any given session, the evaluation order is: (1) arm check, (2) exit check, (3) disarm priority over exit. Same-day arm+exit is explicitly supported and occurred on Jun 18, 2026 (exit at $639.45). A deferred-price bug where the exit was incorrectly assigned the Jun 11 price (~$586.93 rather than $639.45) was identified and fixed. Tests V1a–V1c lock the exit date, exit price, and no-trade window (Jun 9–12).

P2 — on20 Momentum Sizing Tilt

on20_mom = on20 − on20.shift(5): the 5-session change in overnight stream. When this turns negative in MONITOR state (threshold: −5%), overnight institutional demand is fading, reinforcing the distribution signal. The tilt multiplies suggested_size by 0.8 (20% reduction). Hard constraint: on20_mom never enters transition logic. It is a display and sizing variable only (test V9 enforces this via source inspection). Current reading: −10.8% (fading overnight bid confirmed).

P3 — Short-Permission Indicator

Three conditions must all be true for short permission: (1) close below 50-DMA and volume above 1.5× the 30-day average; (2) id20 < 0; (3) on20 < 0. Currently: condition 2 passes (id20 = −12.2%), conditions 1 and 3 fail. Short not permitted. The nearest actionable line is MA50 at $545 (3.7% below the Jul 2 close of $566).

P4 — Ensemble Arm (ENSEMBLE_ARM, default OFF)

A 2-of-3 majority vote of the hybrid arm condition computed at window lengths N=15, 20, and 25. Against the Jan–Jul 2026 fixture, ENSEMBLE_ARM=True yields a return of ~72.7% vs baseline 72.7% with the same Jun 18 exit date and price. The ensemble adds noise-resistance but does not change outcomes on this sample. Kept as an optional flag (default OFF) pending multi-year calibration. Configuration: ENSEMBLE_ARM = False in state_machine.py.

P5 — Crash Gate (CRASH_GATE, default OFF)

Suppresses the absolute ABS arm (id20 < −3%) when the 20-session drawdown from the rolling high (dd20) is ≤ −10% (i.e., already in a crash). Rationale: arming after a 10%+ collapse produces a knife-edge MONITOR signal that forces an exit at the worst possible price. Against the fixture, CRASH_GATE=True prevents a brief MONITOR signal at the Mar 30, 2026 low (dd20 = −10.3%), allowing participation in the April recovery. YTD return rises from ~72.7% to ~73.0%. Improvement is small on n=1 episode and the threshold is fragile; kept OFF pending multi-year calibration. Configuration: CRASH_GATE = False, CRASH_GATE_DD = −0.10.

P6 — Real Yield Context (real_chg20)

real_chg20 = (DGS10 − T10YIE) − (DGS10 − T10YIE).shift(20): the 20-session change in the real 10-year yield (nominal − breakeven inflation). Sourced from FRED (no API key required). Confirmed useful as a macro context indicator (rising real yields = headwind for growth/tech). Rejected as a sizing overlay: high correlation with RV20 means it would double-count the volatility adjustment already in the sizing formula. Remains a display-only field in the checklist and today output.

Corrected Performance Characterization

The in-sample return of ~72.7% (Jan–Jul 2026, fixture) should be interpreted as follows:

  • The fixture spans a single bull-market year with an unusually sharp crash event and fast recovery. This is exactly the scenario the signal was designed for.
  • The buy-and-hold baseline over the same period is approximately 81% (depending on exact start/end dates). The strategy underperforms B&H on the YTD period because the exit occurred at $639 and the Jul 2 close is $566 — i.e., the strategy correctly avoided the drawdown but the period has not yet closed out (the market is still below the exit price).
  • Performance is expressed as equity-curve terminal value (unrealized P&L since exit counts as cash-at-0%), not as closed-trade P&L.
  • There are exactly 3 trades. A 3-trade sample is insufficient for statistical inference about expected performance across regimes. Do not extrapolate.

Additional Notes from v3.2

  • EXIT_ID threshold: The exec-into-strength threshold EXIT_ID = 0.01 (+1% intraday) is empirically derived from the Jun 18 data. A lower threshold (e.g., 0.5%) would have exited Jun 10 at ~$595; a higher threshold (2%) would have missed the Jun 18 window. Treat as fragile.
  • Calibration gap: All constants (ARM_DIV_ID, ARM_Z, COLLAPSE_GATE, EXIT_ID, ACCUM_STOP_PX, etc.) were set or confirmed on n=3 episodes spanning a single regime (post-AI bull market 2025–2026). They have not been walked forward on 2019–2024 data. Pre-2019 SOXX data is sparser and the ETF composition has changed materially. Multi-year calibration is the highest-priority future work.
  • FRED data: VIXCLS, VXVCLS, DGS10, T10YIE are fetched from the FRED graph CSV API (no key required). vix_slope and real_chg20 are computed from these. If FRED is unavailable, the pipeline continues with null values for these fields — they are context-only and do not affect state machine transitions.
